good collaboration

December 21, 2019

Many institutes have policies and guidelines for collborative studies. What other criteria research leaders consider when starting a collaborative studies? There are some criteria in a paper published by Professor Michael Parker in 2016.

Here a brief note is provided.

1- Engaging the collaborators in the study conception and the intellectual work.
2- Requesting for the information about the patients and high-quality samples according to a timetable.
3- Ensuring high quality research with excellent proposal.
4- Providing opportunities for the researchers in develping countries to visit the institute and receive training if needed.
5- Asking for team members about their motives and their policies for collaboration.
6- Providing opportunities for team members to voice their concerns about different aspects of the research.
7- Building confidence by involving all team members in research and updating them about the progress of the research and results.
8- Understanding their contribution in the research and acknowledging it.
